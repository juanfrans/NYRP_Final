HAVEN PROJECT - METRICS

Analyzing health and environmental metrics for New York City Neighborhoods. A project by New York Restoration Project and the Spatial Information Design Lab.

This is a work in progress...

Through these maps and charts we attempt to analyze and compare different neighborhoods in New York City according to specific health and environmental metrics. This site serves as a tool for the Haven Project, a collaboration between multiple organizations led by the New York Restoration Project.

NYC OVERALL METRICS

The metrics on this first map serve to compare different neighborhoods in New York City. We used three types of measurements:

  • Environmental, which includes tree and grass coverage, pollution (PM 2.5) levels and walking distance to recreational spaces measuring 6 acres or more.
  • Health, including asthma rates, self-reported exercise, and percentage of overweight people.
  • Demographics, which includes percentage of the population below the poverty line, percentage of the population younger than 18 and older than 65, and percentage of the population having attained only high-school or less.
